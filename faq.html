FAQ

Everything you want to know about your Himalayan trek.

What is the best time for Himalayan trekking?

Uttarakhand offers trekking opportunities throughout the year, but the best time depends on the specific trek:

  • Summer (April to June): Perfect for high-altitude treks like Roopkund and Bali Pass. The weather is pleasant and trails are clear.
  • Monsoon (July to August): The only time to witness the Valley of Flowers in full bloom, though other treks may be closed due to terrain risks.
  • Post-Monsoon (Sept to Nov): Offers the sharpest peak views and best photography conditions.
  • Winter (Dec to March): The ideal season for dedicated snow treks like Kedarkantha or Brahmatal.
Are your treks safe for beginners?

Absolutely! We have a curated selection of treks specifically designed for first-timers, such as Nag Tibba and Chopta Tungnath. These routes feature gradual ascents and well-maintained paths. Our expert guides are trained in wilderness first aid and provide constant support, ensuring a safe and memorable experience regardless of your prior hiking background.

What kind of food do you provide on the trek?

We pride ourselves on serving fresh, nutritious, and primarily vegetarian meals prepared by our onsite trek cooks. A typical menu includes:

  • Breakfast: Porridge, eggs, toast, seasonal fruits, and hot tea/coffee.
  • Lunch: Balanced meals with rice, seasonal vegetables, dal, and rotis.
  • Dinner: Multi-course warm meals followed by a dessert.

We use locally sourced ingredients and can accommodate specific dietary needs (like vegan or gluten-free) if informed during booking.

Do I need to carry my own heavy backpack?

While we encourage trekkers to carry their own gear for the full experience, we offer backpack offloading services. You can pay an additional fee to have your main rucksack (up to 10kg) carried by a mule or porter. In this case, you only need to carry a small daypack containing essentials like water, snacks, camera, and rain gear.

How do you handle medical emergencies on the trail?

Safety is our absolute priority. Our trek leaders carry a comprehensive medical kit, including oxygen cylinders and oximeters. Every guide is certified in Basic Life Support and trained to recognize symptoms of Altitude Sickness (AMS). In rare cases of severe injury or illness, we have established protocols for immediate evacuation via mule or helicopter to the nearest medical facility.

Is drinking water safe during the trek?

We provide water sourced from pristine mountain springs, which is then boiled or treated with water purification tablets to ensure it's safe for consumption. We strongly discourage the use of single-use plastic bottles. Please bring your own reusable water bottles (at least 2 matching 1-liter capacity) to refill at our campsites.
